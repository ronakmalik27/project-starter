# ADR-0000: Title (short imperative, e.g. "Use Postgres as the single datastore")

- Status: Proposed | Accepted | Superseded by ADR-XXXX
- Date: YYYY-MM-DD
- Deciders: <who>

## Context

What forces are at play: the requirement, the constraints, the options on the
table, and why this decision needs making now. State the facts a future reader
needs to understand the choice without you in the room.

## Decision

The choice, stated plainly in one or two sentences. Name the industry-standard
practice this matches or beats (see docs/process/00-principles.md); if it sits
below par, justify that here.

## Consequences

What becomes easier, what becomes harder, and what this commits us to. Include
the follow-on work and the design hooks a deferred feature needs now (so adding
it later is not a rewrite).

## Alternatives considered

Each realistic alternative, in a sentence or two, and why it lost. An ADR with
no rejected alternatives usually means the decision was not really made.
